Remote Sensing

Remote sensing involves the collection of information about Earth's surface from a distance, primarily through satellite and aerial imagery. Key aspects include:
  • Satellite Imagery: Capturing images of the Earth from space using satellites. These images are used for various purposes, including monitoring environmental changes, weather forecasting, and resource management.
  • Aerial Photography: Using aircraft to capture detailed images of the surface. Drones have become increasingly popular for more localized and precise imagery.
  • Sensor Technology: Devices that measure reflected or emitted electromagnetic energy. These sensors can detect various wavelengths of light, allowing scientists to study different aspects of the environment.
  • Data Processing and Analysis: Transforming raw image data into useful information through techniques like image classification, spectral analysis, and change detection.
Remote sensing is crucial for understanding environmental trends, tracking natural disasters, and managing land and resources effectively.
